How It Works

Text-passage search scans against CHGIS names, including simplified/traditional variants and short administrative aliases. Place-name search uses the names you enter directly.

Detected candidates are shown beside the map and can be unchecked. Date and level filters apply before candidates are mapped.

Administrative labels shift over time. The upper-level option groups records such as 郡, 州, 府, and 路 without assuming they meant the same thing in every period.

Notes

  • Use traditional Chinese for manual place-name searches when possible.
  • Separate manual names with commas, either "," or "，".
  • Nearby or identical locations are grouped into one marker with all CHGIS records in the popup.
  • CHGIS links in popups open the corresponding placename search.
