Simple CHGIS Map



OR

If you're reading a historical text and you want to quickly see where the places mentioned therein were, here's a simple way.

In the "Place name" box, you can type individual place names (use traditional Chinese 繁體中文) or a list of places, separated by commas (regular ',' or Chinese '，').

You can specify a year in the Western calendar or leave it blank. You can also leave the place name blank to retrieve all places for a given year.

By default, the map will show both prefectures (jun 郡 and equivalent) and counties (xian 縣 and equivalent).

Data is from the China Historical Geographical Information System (CHGIS, v6). You can also try out their API: https://maps.cga.harvard.edu/tgaz/?.

Scrolling over the points shows you the name of the feature and the relevant dates, along with CHGIS information about how it started and ended.

Clicking on a point will give you a link to a CHGIS page where you can find more information, including their discussions of the historical sources.

Nearby and overlapping points will collapse; the number shows how many there are. Click to unfold and zoom in.
