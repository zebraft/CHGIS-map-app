Simple CHGIS Map



OR

If you're reading a historical text and you want to quickly see where the places (prefectures 郡 and counties 縣, and their administrative equivalents) mentioned therein were, here's a simple tool.

Data is from the China Historical Geographical Information System (CHGIS, v6).

In the "Place name" box, you can type individual place names (use traditional Chinese 繁體中文) or a list of places, separated by commas (regular ',' or Chinese '，').

You can specify a year (or range of years) in the Western calendar. Or you can leave the date range blank. Likewise, you can leave the place name blank to retrieve all places for a given year.

By default, the map will show both prefectures and counties. Untick one of the boxes to see one of those levels alone.

Scrolling over a point will reveal the name of the feature and the relevant dates, along with CHGIS information (as available) about how it started and ended.

Clicking on a point will give you a link to a CHGIS page with more information, including their discussions of the historical sources.

Nearby and overlapping points will collapse; the number shows how many there are. Click to unfold and zoom in.
